DEFINITION

Option- A financial derivative that represents a contract sold by one party to another party. The contract offers the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy (call) or sell (put) a security or other financial assets (underlying) at an agreed-upon price (strike price) during a certain time period (time to expiration) or on a specific date (expiration date).

A call option gives the option holder the right to buy the underlying asset by a certain specified date for a specified price.

A put option gives the option holder the right to sell the underlying asset by a selected date for a pre-selected price.

Black and Scholes In 1973, Fischer Black and Myron Scholes published their groundbreaking paper the pricing of options and corporate liabilities. Not only did this specify the first successful options pricing formula, but it also described a general framework for pricing other derivative instruments. That paper launched the field of financial engineering.

The Black-Scholes (1973) option pricing formula prices European put or call options on a stock that does not pay a dividend or make other distributions. The formula assumes the underlying stock price follows a geometric Brownian motion with constant volatility.

We should first understand the factors which influence the change in the price of an option. Then we can better understand how this fits in with the Option Greeks.

The 5 Option Greeks measure the sensitivity of the price of stock options in relation to 4 different factors; Changes in the underlying stock price, interest rate, volatility and time decay.

Option Greeks allow option traders to objectively calculate changes in the value of the option contracts in their portfolio with changes in the factors that affects the value of stock options. The ability to mathematically calculate these changes gives option traders the ability to hedge their portfolio or to construct positions with specific risk/reward profiles.

BINOMIAL TREE

The binomial lethbridge.model breaks down the time to expiration into potentially a very large number of time intervals, or steps. A tree of stock prices is initially produced working forward from the present to expiration. At each step it is assumed that the stock price will move up or down by an amount calculated using volatility and time to expiration. This produces a binomial distribution, or recombining tree, of underlying stock prices. The tree represents all the possible paths that the stock price could take during the life of the option.

At the end of the tree, which is at expiration of the option, all the terminal option prices for each of the final possible stock prices are known as they simply equal their intrinsic values.

Next the option prices at each step of the tree are calculated working back from expiration to the present. The option prices at each step are used to derive the option prices at the next step of the tree using risk neutral valuation based on the probabilities of the stock prices moving up or down, the risk free rate and the time interval of each step. Any adjustments to stock prices at an ex-dividend date or option prices as a result of early exercise of american options are worked into the calculations at the required point in time. At the top of the tree you are left with one option price.

Help

Within the help section, users can find the reference for the parameters shown in OSA system.

Exercise Price = (Strike Price) The fixed price defined by the option contract at which the option may be exercised. The owner of the option can either purchase (call option) or sell (put option) the underlying instrument at this fixed price.

Underlying Price = The market price of the underlying asset. A change in the underlying price leads to a simultaneous change in the price of the option.

Time to Expiration = The time prior to the expiration date of the option contract. After the expiration date, the contract is either not valuable or cannot be exercised. In OSA system, we use "day" as the unit of Time to Expiration with a year of 365-day basis.

Risk Free Rate = Rate of return from a risk free investment.

Volatility = (Historical Volatility) Measures the volatility of the underlying instrument. The volatility is an input to the option valuation lethbridge.model and is based on actual, recent price changes of the underlying.

Implied Volatility = The implied volatility of an option contract is the volatility of the price of the underlying asset that is implied by the market price of the option based on an option pricing lethbridge.model. It is calculated using the market price of an option, along with the parameters used for option valuation lethbridge.model, in effect by working backwards to solve the price equation.

Market Price = The market price of the option contract, with which we calculate the implied volatility. It is normally provided by instructor or from market curNews.
